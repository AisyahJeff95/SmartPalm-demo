It can show real Earth imagery, but what I made currently shows **quicklook previews** from Copernicus, not full-resolution analysis-ready imagery.

For Sentinel:

- **Sentinel-2** is optical imagery. This can look like real satellite photos of land, clouds, coastlines, vegetation, cities, etc.
- **Sentinel-1** is radar imagery. It is real Earth observation data, but it does not look like a normal photograph. It often appears grayscale or false-color because it measures radar backscatter, not visible light.
- The map background in the viewer is OpenStreetMap, not Sentinel imagery.
- The product “quicklooks” are real preview images when Copernicus provides them, but they are low-resolution browse images.

So: **not illustrations**, but also **not yet full-resolution Sentinel image rendering**.

To show actual Sentinel-2 image tiles more like a satellite photo, the next step would be to add a true-color render using bands:

- `B04` red
- `B03` green
- `B02` blue

For Sentinel-1, we would render radar bands such as `VV` and `VH` as grayscale or false color.
